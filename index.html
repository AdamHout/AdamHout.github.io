Modified Caesar Cipher

Platform: Linux
Language: C

A Caesar cipher is an ancient form of encryption alleged to have been used by Julius Caesar. It is a plaintext substitution cipher that works by replacing each character in a message with another character a fixed number of positions down the alphabet. For example, a shift of three postions would transpose the letter 'A' to the letter 'D'. A shift of the letter 'Y' by three positions results in the letter 'B'. My modfied version of this cipher deviates from the traditional Caesar cipher in two ways:

  1. Rather than shifting characters by a fixed number of positions, I use the low order nibble of the previous character as the shift value for the current character to be transposed.
  2. A Caesar cipher is typically restricted to the 26 letters of the alphabet in either all upper or all lower case. My logic employs the entire pritinable range of the ASCII table i.e. 0x20 - 0x7E. This allows the use of upper and lower case letters, numbers and punctuation characters.
